 The most dramatic this year occurred in Ingushetia, a region adjoining Chechnya, when guerrillas ambushed and decimated a convoy of about 20 Russian troops. Russian leaders, from Mr. Putin down, have been declaring the Chechen war over and the region pacified since February. But ambushes and nighttime exchanges of fire remain common, and most outside experts say the region is destined to suffer a long war of attrition.
